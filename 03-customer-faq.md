# Apex Auto Insurance — Customer FAQ

## General Questions

**Q: Does my policy cover me if I drive someone else's car?**
A: Yes, in most cases. Auto insurance generally follows the car, not the driver. If you drive a friend's car with their permission and have an accident, their insurance pays first. Your Apex policy acts as secondary coverage if their limits are exhausted. However, if you regularly drive someone else's car, they should add you to their policy.

**Q: Does my policy cover someone else driving my car?**
A: Yes, if you give them permission (this is called "permissive use"). Your Apex policy covers occasional drivers using your car with your consent. However, people who regularly drive your car should be listed on your policy. Excluded drivers are never covered.

**Q: Am I covered when I rent a car?**
A: If you have collision and comprehensive on your Apex policy, that coverage typically extends to rental cars within the U.S. You may not need to purchase the rental company's collision damage waiver (CDW). Call Apex at 1-800-APEX-AUTO to confirm before renting. International rentals are not covered.

**Q: What happens if an uninsured driver hits me?**
A: If you have Uninsured Motorist (UM) coverage on your Apex policy, we will pay for your medical bills and vehicle repairs up to your UM limits. If you do not have UM coverage, you would need to pursue the at-fault driver personally, which can be very difficult if they have no assets. Apex strongly recommends adding UM/UIM coverage.

**Q: Does my policy cover rideshare driving (Uber/Lyft)?**
A: Standard personal auto policies exclude rideshare activity while the app is active. Apex offers a Rideshare Endorsement for $8/month that fills the coverage gap. Without this endorsement, you may be uninsured during Period 1 (app on, waiting for a ride request). Uber and Lyft provide their own coverage during Periods 2 and 3 (en route to pickup and during trip).

**Q: What if I hit a deer?**
A: Hitting an animal is covered under Comprehensive coverage, not Collision. This is an important distinction because comprehensive deductibles are often lower than collision deductibles. Make sure to file the claim under comprehensive.

**Q: Does my insurance cover flood damage?**
A: Yes — flood, hurricane, and water damage are covered under Comprehensive coverage, not Collision. If your car is damaged in a flood, file a comprehensive claim.

**Q: Is my car covered if it catches fire?**
A: Yes. Fire damage is covered under Comprehensive coverage.

## Premium & Billing Questions

**Q: How can I lower my premium?**
A: Several ways to reduce your Apex premium:
- Increase your deductible (higher deductible = lower premium)
- Bundle with Apex home insurance (save up to 15%)
- Maintain a clean driving record
- Complete a defensive driving course (save up to 10%)
- Install a telematics device through Apex DriveSmarter program (save up to 20%)
- Pay annually instead of monthly (save 5%)
- Add safety features: anti-theft devices, anti-lock brakes, backup cameras

**Q: Why did my premium go up?**
A: Common reasons for premium increases:
- An at-fault accident on your record
- A traffic violation (speeding ticket, DUI)
- Adding a young or inexperienced driver to the policy
- Your vehicle's repair costs increased industry-wide
- You moved to a higher-risk zip code
- Your credit score decreased (in states where credit scoring is permitted)

**Q: When is my payment due?**
A: Apex bills on the same date each month as your policy start date. You can set up autopay through the Apex mobile app or customer portal. A 10-day grace period applies before cancellation for non-payment.

## Claims Questions

**Q: How long does a claim take?**
A: Simple claims (minor fender-bender, glass repair): 3–5 business days. Moderate claims (vehicle damage requiring repair): 1–3 weeks depending on repair shop availability. Total loss claims: 2–4 weeks for settlement. Complex claims involving injuries or liability disputes: 30–90 days or longer.

**Q: Will filing a claim raise my rates?**
A: At-fault claims typically increase your premium at renewal. Not-at-fault claims (someone hit you) and comprehensive claims (weather, theft) generally do not affect your premium. Apex offers Accident Forgiveness — your first at-fault accident is forgiven after 5 years of clean driving.

**Q: What is a deductible and when do I pay it?**
A: Your deductible is the amount you pay out-of-pocket before insurance covers the rest. You pay it directly to the repair shop, not to Apex. Example: $5,000 in damage with a $500 deductible — Apex pays $4,500, you pay $500. Deductibles apply to collision and comprehensive claims. They do NOT apply to liability claims or UM/UIM bodily injury claims.

**Q: Can I choose my own repair shop?**
A: Yes. You may use any licensed repair shop. Apex also has a network of Apex Certified Repair Centers that offer guaranteed workmanship and streamlined claims processing. Using a Certified Repair Center often speeds up the claim.

## Coverage Scenario Questions

**Q: I parked on the street and someone hit my car and drove off. Am I covered?**
A: Yes — a hit-and-run where the other driver fled is covered under Uninsured Motorist Property Damage (UMPD) if you have that coverage, or under Collision coverage. File a police report as soon as possible, as it is required for hit-and-run claims.

**Q: My teenager just got their license. Do I need to add them?**
A: Yes. Any licensed driver in your household must be listed on your policy. Failing to add a household driver can result in claim denial. Teen drivers typically increase premiums; Apex offers a Good Student Discount (3.0 GPA or higher) that can offset some of the increase.

**Q: My car was broken into and my laptop was stolen. Am I covered?**
A: The damage to your car (broken window, damaged lock) is covered under Comprehensive. However, the laptop and any personal property inside the vehicle are NOT covered by auto insurance. Personal belongings are covered under homeowners or renters insurance.
