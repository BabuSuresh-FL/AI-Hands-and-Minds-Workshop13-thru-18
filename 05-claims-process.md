# Apex Auto Insurance — Claims Process Guide

## How to File a Claim with Apex

### Step 1: Ensure Safety First
After an accident, move to a safe location if possible. Check for injuries. Call 911 if anyone is injured or if the accident involves significant property damage.

### Step 2: Gather Information at the Scene
Collect the following from all parties involved:
- Full name, address, phone number
- Driver's license number
- License plate number
- Insurance company name and policy number
- Vehicle make, model, year, and color
- Photos of all vehicles, damage, and the accident scene
- Names and contact information of any witnesses
- Police report number (if police responded)

### Step 3: File Your Claim
File your Apex claim through any of these channels:
- **Apex Mobile App**: Fastest method — upload photos directly
- **Online**: www.apexautoinsurance.com/claims
- **Phone**: 1-800-APEX-CLAIM (24/7 claims hotline)
- **Agent**: Contact your local Apex agent directly

File as soon as possible. Ohio law requires claims to be reported promptly. Delayed reporting can complicate your claim.

### Step 4: Claims Assignment
Within 24 hours of filing, Apex assigns a dedicated Claims Adjuster who will:
- Contact you to review the details
- Explain the next steps
- Answer your coverage questions
- Schedule vehicle inspection if needed

### Step 5: Vehicle Inspection and Damage Assessment
- **Apex Certified Repair Center**: Drive-in for same-day or next-day inspection
- **Mobile Adjuster**: Apex sends an adjuster to your location
- **Photo Estimate**: Upload photos via the Apex app for minor damage

### Step 6: Repair or Settlement
**If Repairable**:
- Apex issues a repair authorization to your chosen shop
- You pay your deductible directly to the repair shop
- Apex pays the shop directly for the remainder

**If Total Loss**:
- Apex determines Actual Cash Value (ACV) using market data
- You receive ACV minus your deductible
- Apex handles the title transfer

### Step 7: Rental Car (if applicable)
If you have Rental Reimbursement coverage, Apex authorizes a rental car starting the day after you drop off your vehicle for repair. Call the claims hotline to activate rental coverage.

## Claim Types and What to Expect

### Collision Claim
- **When**: You hit another vehicle or object, or are hit by another vehicle
- **Deductible applies**: Yes
- **Fault matters**: Yes — at-fault claims may affect your premium
- **Timeline**: 5–14 business days for repair completion

### Comprehensive Claim
- **When**: Theft, weather, fire, animal strike, vandalism, falling objects
- **Deductible applies**: Yes (usually lower than collision deductible)
- **Fault matters**: No — comprehensive claims typically do not affect your premium
- **Timeline**: 3–10 business days

### Liability Claim (someone else files against you)
- **When**: You caused an accident and the other party files a claim
- **Deductible applies**: No — liability claims have no deductible
- **Your role**: Cooperate with Apex's investigation; do not admit fault
- **Timeline**: 30–90 days depending on complexity

### Uninsured Motorist Claim
- **When**: Hit by an uninsured driver or hit-and-run
- **Requirements**: Police report strongly recommended; required for hit-and-run
- **Deductible applies**: Varies by state — in Ohio, typically no deductible for UM bodily injury
- **Timeline**: 2–6 weeks

## After Your Claim: What Happens to Your Premium?

| Claim Type | Premium Impact |
|---|---|
| At-fault collision | Likely increase at renewal |
| Not-at-fault collision | Typically no increase |
| Comprehensive (weather, theft) | Typically no increase |
| Uninsured motorist | Typically no increase |
| Second at-fault in 3 years | Significant increase, possible non-renewal |

Apex's **Accident Forgiveness** program waives the premium increase for your first at-fault accident if you have been accident-free for 5+ years.

## Fraud Warning
Insurance fraud is a felony in Ohio. Staging accidents, inflating claims, or misrepresenting facts to obtain insurance benefits can result in criminal prosecution, policy cancellation, and being banned from purchasing insurance. Apex actively investigates suspicious claims with the Ohio Department of Insurance Fraud Unit.
