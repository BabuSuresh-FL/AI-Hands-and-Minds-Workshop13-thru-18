# Ohio Auto Insurance Requirements — Apex Guide

## Ohio Minimum Coverage Requirements

Ohio law requires all drivers to carry a minimum level of auto insurance. Driving without insurance in Ohio is illegal and results in serious penalties.

### Ohio Minimum Liability Limits (25/50/25)
- **$25,000** Bodily Injury per person
- **$50,000** Bodily Injury per accident
- **$25,000** Property Damage per accident

These are the bare minimums. Apex strongly recommends higher limits, as a serious accident can easily exceed these amounts and leave you personally liable for the difference.

### Is Ohio a No-Fault State?
No. Ohio is an **at-fault** (tort) state. This means the driver who causes the accident is responsible for paying damages. The injured party can sue the at-fault driver for damages exceeding insurance limits.

### Is PIP Required in Ohio?
No. Personal Injury Protection (PIP) is not required in Ohio. However, Apex recommends adding Medical Payments (MedPay) coverage to help with your own medical bills after an accident, regardless of fault.

### Proof of Insurance in Ohio
Ohio drivers must carry proof of insurance at all times. Acceptable forms include:
- Insurance ID card (physical or digital on your phone)
- Policy declarations page
- Apex mobile app — displays your insurance ID card

### Ohio Penalties for Driving Without Insurance
- First offense: License and registration suspension, $160 reinstatement fee
- Second offense: Suspension + SR-22 requirement for 3 years + $360 reinstatement fee
- Third offense: Suspension + SR-22 + $660 reinstatement fee + possible vehicle immobilization
- All offenses: Must purchase SR-22 filing to prove future financial responsibility

### SR-22 Filing
If you are required to file an SR-22 in Ohio, Apex can file it electronically on your behalf. An SR-22 is a certificate of financial responsibility, not a type of insurance. It typically costs $25–$50 to file and remains on your policy for the required period (usually 3 years).

## Ohio Uninsured Motorist Statistics
Approximately 12.4% of Ohio drivers are uninsured. Apex strongly recommends adding UM/UIM coverage to protect yourself from these drivers.

## Ohio Total Loss Rules
Ohio follows the "total loss threshold" rule. If repair costs exceed 75% of the vehicle's actual cash value (ACV), the vehicle is declared a total loss. Apex will pay you the ACV of your vehicle minus your deductible.
